# LinkedIn post draft

After the better part of a year of pair-programming full-time with AI
coding agents, I noticed I was burning hours every week on the same six
failures — agents
forgetting context between sessions, plans evaporating after compact,
multiple agents racing to lead the same task, time estimates frozen at
human-engineer hours.

The fix wasn't better prompts. These are coordination problems, not prompt
problems.

So I built AiPlus — five small Rust modules that give an AI coding
workflow what a real engineering team already has: memory that survives
sessions, division of labor across roles, calibrated time accounting, and
an auditor that's actually independent of the builder.

The honest meta-frame: I used AI agents to build the toolchain that
manages AI agents.

Fully local. Nothing uploads. Apache-2.0.

https://github.com/izhiwen/aiplus
